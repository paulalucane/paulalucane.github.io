Illustrated Book Cover

This is an illustrated book cover for Toshikazu Kawaguchi's Before the Coffee Gets Cold.

This project was created as part of content creation course at Jönköping University. I chose this specific book because it is one of my favourites and the themes explored in the story felt especially inspirational. Read more about the creation process below!

Created with: ProCreate, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

This novel is set in Tokyo, Japan, in a small café. It follows different characters, all somehow connected, as they travel back in time to visit their loved ones from the past. There is just one rule they absolutely have to remember, they have to return to the present before the coffee gets cold.

Concept and Themes

To represent the title of the book and the central theme of coffee and the limited time I Illustrated a coffee cup, the steam of which forms the watch hands showing the time. The hours are not represented by real numbers but just by two lines, as the concept of time in this novel is a little warped and unclear.

Additional themes of this book are love, relationships, regret and grief, and, of course, a little bit of magic in the ordinary. I chose darker shades of pink for love, blue for grief, the brown for coffee and the dark wood furniture inside the café. The overall color palette is representative of the vintage, heartfelt, cosy, and a little dark aesthetic of the story.

Japanese Influence

When illustrating the coffee cup I searched for inspiration in Japanese china design. I came across the Japanese art of kintsugi, where a piece of ceramic has been repaired with visible golden joins. This method celebrates imperfection and the history of the object.

I thought it was an excellent fit for the cover as it visually represents the healing and transformation that the characters go through as they return back to present, after visiting the past. It also represents the passing of time, and is, of course, connected to coffee.

For the background, I took inspiration from vintage Japanese wallpaper and included the illustrations of cherry blossoms, cranes, Mount Fuji and a temple silhouette.
